Two-way Communication for Impaired People

VISION is a project aimed at enhancing communication for people with impairments, leveraging the power of Python programming. The project focuses on developing a two-way communication system that facilitates interaction between impaired individuals and others in a more accessible and efficient manner. By integrating technologies such as speech recognition, text-to-speech, and sign language recognition, VISION enables users to communicate effectively, whether they are hearing, speech, or visually impaired.

One of the key components of VISION is speech recognition, which enables users to communicate by converting spoken words into text. This is particularly useful for individuals who have difficulty speaking or those who are deaf or hard of hearing. The system transcribes speech from other people in real time, allowing users to read and understand conversations. It also utilizes text-to-speech technology, enabling the system to vocalize text for individuals with visual impairments. This feature ensures that users can access information and participate in discussions with ease, even if they cannot read text on a screen.

What is VISION?

VISION is an innovative tool that bridges communication gaps for impaired individuals, focusing on converting sign language to text or speech, enabling effortless interaction with others. Another vital feature of VISION is sign language recognition. Using machine learning and computer vision, the system interprets sign language gestures and converts them into text or speech. This feature bridges the gap for those who rely on sign language as their primary mode of communication. By using real-time camera input, the system can recognize various sign language gestures and translate them into spoken or written words, fostering smoother communication between sign language users and non-sign language speakers.
